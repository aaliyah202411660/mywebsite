Why Climate Action Matters?

Climate action is crucial because climate change poses a serious threat to life on Earth. It affects every part of our planet—from the air we breathe to the food we eat, the water we drink, and the weather we experience. Taking action against climate change is not just about protecting the environment; it’s also about ensuring a better future for people, animals, and ecosystems.

What Causes Climate Change?

Climate change is mainly caused by human activities that release greenhouse gases into the atmosphere. Burning fossil fuels like coal, oil, and natural gas for energy and transportation produces large amounts of carbon dioxide and other harmful gases. Deforestation also plays a big role because trees that absorb carbon dioxide are being cut down, leading to more carbon in the air. Industrial processes, agriculture, and waste also contribute to the release of greenhouse gases. These gases trap heat in the Earth’s atmosphere, causing global temperatures to rise.

What Are Ways to Solve Climate Change?

There are many ways we can fight climate change. One of the most important is switching to clean, renewable energy sources like solar, wind, and hydro power instead of fossil fuels. Planting more trees and protecting forests help absorb carbon dioxide from the atmosphere. People can also help by using less energy, reducing waste, recycling, and choosing environmentally friendly products. Governments play a key role by making laws and policies that support green technologies and limit pollution.
